Matchmaker: A Generic Framework for Consumer-Producer Matching

Overview

This project aims to create a generic framework to match consumers of a product with its producers, à la Uber or AirBnB. The reason such a framework might be desirable is that it could free producers from the hegemony of the network provider, by allowing then to easily bring up their own matching service.

It is a hypothesis of the project that this family of services have enough in common that a generic framework can cover a large enough portion of what goes on that creating a specific service can be done (mostly?) by non-programmers using templates. So, we are looking at providing "Platform-as-a-Service" (PaaS), with an open-source core.

Financing Model

Taking our cue from previously successful projects of this type, such as GitHub, Docker, Kubernetes, Jira, etc., our model will be to keep the whole core of the system open source, and basic use of it free. This will assure potential users that they are not "locking in" to a new proprietary platform by choosing to commit to our PaaS. Instead, revenue will be derived from:

  • Providing a fee-based hosting environment that will make putting up a "matchmaker" service extremely easy; and
  • Selling consulting and other specialized services as add-ons to the freely avialable code base.
